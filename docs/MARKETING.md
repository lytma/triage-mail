# Triage Mail — Go-to-Market Plan

## Positioning

For solo consultants and freelancers who juggle multiple Gmail and Outlook inboxes and need to find what actually deserves attention, Triage Mail is an AI email client that reads every incoming message and routes it into a short prioritized Review queue — so you only open the mail that matters. Unlike Superhuman, Hey, Spark, or Gmail's built-in tabs, Triage Mail's AI actually reads and classifies every email individually, not just sender-based filtering.

## Value proposition

Stop scanning your inbox. Triage Mail's AI reads every incoming email across all your Gmail and Outlook accounts, decides what actually needs you, and puts only that in a short Review queue. Everything else — newsletters, marketing, receipts, notifications — gets filed into folders you can skim or bulk-clear in seconds. You write plain-English rules that always override the AI, you see a reason and confidence level on every item, and your email body content is never stored. The result: you open a prioritized list of what matters, not a raw inbox.

## Differentiation

The core differentiator is that the AI reads and classifies every individual email — not sender-based heuristics, not tab rules, not priority inbox flags. Gmail's tabs sort by sender patterns; Superhuman and Spark use shortcuts and sender-based AI features but don't read and classify every message with a per-item reason and confidence score. Hey filters by screening rules. Triage Mail combines per-email AI classification with plain-English user rules that always override the AI, a unified Review queue across all mailboxes, and metadata-only storage (no email body persisted). Against doing nothing — continuing to manually scan multiple inboxes — Triage Mail turns 30+ minutes of daily scanning into a short prioritized queue you clear in minutes.

## Messaging

### Your AI reads every email. You only open what matters.

**Audience:** Solo consultants & freelancers

- Connect Gmail and Outlook — one unified Review queue of what actually needs you.
- AI classifies every email individually: important, FYI, newsletters, marketing, receipts, notifications.
- Write plain-English rules: 'Anything from my accountant is always important.' Rules always win.
- See a reason and confidence level on every item so you can trust the system.
- Your email body is never stored — only metadata. Privacy by design.

### Stop scanning. Start triaging.

**Audience:** High-volume knowledge workers

- Keyboard-first: J/K to navigate, R to reply, E to archive, Shift+E to mark done.
- Review queue ordered by importance then recency — the most urgent item is always on top.
- Low-confidence items are flagged, not hidden — you see them when skimming categories.
- Bulk-archive marketing and newsletters in one click. Two-way sync keeps Gmail/Outlook consistent.
- Triage stats show your queue clearance rate and how much the AI is handling for you.

### One queue for all your inboxes. Only the important stuff.

**Audience:** Small-business owners

- Unify Gmail and Outlook into a single prioritized Review queue across all accounts.
- Client emails surface to the top; vendor promos and receipts file themselves away.
- Plain-English rules guarantee specific senders are always treated the way you want.
- Get push notifications only when important mail lands — not for every newsletter.
- Two-way sync means everything you do in Triage Mail shows up in your original mailbox.

## Launch plan

### Pre-launch (Weeks 1–4)

**Goal:** Prepare all launch assets, seed the demo, and build a small warm list from zero.

- Finalize the demo account with 50+ realistic seeded emails across all categories, at least 10 important items in the Review queue, and 2 sample triage rules.
- Record a 60-second Loom video showing the Review queue clearing 15–20 emails in under a minute with keyboard shortcuts.
- Build the landing page with demo-first flow, privacy callout, pricing ($12/mo, $108/yr, 14-day free trial no CC), and FAQ.
- Join 5–8 communities (Indie Hackers, r/productivity, r/freelance, r/smallbusiness, r/email, 2 Slack groups). Spend 2 weeks contributing genuinely before mentioning the product.
- Identify 10–15 solo consultants from communities for design-partner outreach. Prepare a personal outreach template: 'I'm building an AI email triage tool for consultants juggling multiple inboxes. Would you be willing to try it for 2 weeks and give me honest feedback? Free access, no strings.'
- Set up basic analytics (Plausible or Fathom — lightweight, privacy-friendly) to track landing page visits, demo opens, trial signups, and mailbox connections.

### Launch (Weeks 5–6)

**Goal:** Generate initial awareness and drive 200–500 demo visitors and 10–20 trial signups via Product Hunt and community posts.

- Launch on Product Hunt on a Tuesday or Wednesday (highest traffic days). Post early morning PT. Have maker comment ready with the personal founder story.
- Post 'Show HN' on Hacker News with a technically honest writeup of the AI triage architecture and privacy decisions.
- Publish the Indie Hackers long-form post on launch day or the day after.
- Email the 10–15 identified design partners with personal invitations and extended 30-day trial links.
- Monitor demo performance and landing page analytics in real time. Be ready to fix any demo bugs within hours.
- Respond to every comment and question on Product Hunt, HN, and community posts within 2 hours during launch day.

### First 90 days (Weeks 5–18)

**Goal:** Recruit 10–15 active design partners, validate the core value proposition, and iterate on triage accuracy and onboarding based on real usage data.

- Personally onboard each design partner with a 15-minute call or Loom walkthrough. Help them connect their first mailbox and set up 2–3 triage rules during onboarding.
- Check in with each design partner weekly via email: 'How's the Review queue working? Anything misclassified? Any rules you wish you could set?'
- Track the PRD's success metrics from day 1: Daily Review Queue Opens, Activation Rate, Review Queue Clearance Rate, Reply/Forward Rate, Weekly Retention, Triage Rule Adoption. These are inherited from the PRD as the product success targets.
- After 2 weeks of use, ask each design partner for a one-sentence quote and permission to use it. Offer 2 free months for a usable testimonial.
- Publish 3 cornerstone SEO articles during this period (see Content & SEO channel tactics).
- Implement the referral mechanism (1 free month per referred paid subscriber) by week 10.
- By week 12, evaluate: which design partners are daily active users? What's the most common triage-rule pattern? What's the #1 onboarding friction? Use these insights to refine messaging and onboarding.
- Aim to convert 2–3 design partners to paid subscribers by week 16 as early validation of willingness to pay.

## Content & SEO strategy

Content targets the specific search intent of solo consultants and freelancers experiencing inbox overload. Three cornerstone articles in the first 90 days: (1) 'How to manage multiple email accounts without losing your mind' — targets the unified-inbox pain point and naturally positions Triage Mail; (2) 'AI email triage: what it is and when it's worth it' — captures the emerging category search and educates the market; (3) 'Gmail vs Outlook inbox management for freelancers' — targets comparison intent from users already searching for solutions. Build a comparison page ('Triage Mail vs Superhuman vs Spark vs Hey') to capture comparison-search traffic — be honest about where competitors win and where Triage Mail wins. Create a free interactive tool ('How much time are you losing to inbox scanning?') that outputs a personalized estimate and ends with a demo CTA — this is linkable and shareable. Submit the demo to AI-tool directories and productivity roundups. SEO is a slow-burn channel: expect minimal organic traffic in the first 90 days, but these assets compound over 6–12 months. Track which articles and search terms drive demo opens, not just page views.

## Pricing & monetization

Pricing is inherited from the PRD: $12/month or $108/year (effectively 2 months free), with a 14-day free trial requiring no credit card. The go-to-market framing is demo-then-paid: every new visitor encounters the demo account first (no signup, no commitment), then a 14-day trial with real mailboxes, then a paid subscription. The LLM API cost is absorbed by the subscription — users are never metered per email. This is the deliberate business model: a straightforward SaaS subscription with no usage-based pricing complexity. For the first 90 days, the primary goal is design partners, not revenue — non-revenue success is defined as: 10–15 active design partners, at least 5 opening the Review queue daily on weekdays, at least 30% creating a triage rule within their first week, and 2–3 converting to paid by week 16 as early willingness-to-pay validation. The demo account is free and unlimited in sessions (each session lasts 24 hours) to maximize top-of-funnel reach. Community-exclusive extended trials (30 days vs. 14) are used as a lightweight acquisition lever during the cold-start phase.
